## Design Context

### Project

Personal-practice site for **Lucas Sayeg**, a mental-health practitioner (psicólogo / psicanalista) in Brazil. Built on the `next-payload-template` (Next.js 16 + Payload CMS 3, Tailwind v4, shadcn/ui, react-hook-form + zod, sonner, Resend for contact). All user-facing content is **Brazilian Portuguese (pt-BR)**; static UI strings live in `src/messages/pt.json` via `next-intl`.

### Users

The primary visitor is an individual in a vulnerable moment — someone considering therapy for the first time, returning after a break, or evaluating Lucas after a referral. They arrive anxious, often skeptical, and want to know within seconds: _Is this a person I can trust? Will I be safe here? What happens if I reach out?_

Secondary visitors: family members researching on someone else's behalf, professionals making referrals, and journalists / institutional contacts. The site has to work for all three without diluting the primary tone.

The job to be done is small and concrete: **read enough to feel reassured, then make first contact** (form or WhatsApp). Long browsing sessions are not the goal; quiet confidence is.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **considered, grounded, literate.**

- **Considered** — every decision feels intentional. Nothing is decorative for its own sake. The visitor senses that the person behind the practice thinks carefully about everything they do.
- **Grounded** — warm without being soft, professional without being clinical. No glossy stock energy, no startup gloss, no clinic-checklist coldness. Adult-to-adult.
- **Literate** — Brazilian psychoanalysis has a deep editorial tradition (Freud, Lacan, Winnicott in translation, the writing of Maria Rita Kehl, Contardo Calligaris). The site should feel adjacent to _that_ world — books, essays, considered prose — rather than wellness-app world.

Emotional goals: **reassurance, dignity, quiet competence.** A visitor in distress should leave the homepage feeling slightly calmer than when they arrived.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial-warm, type-led, lightly handmade.** Imagine a small literary press's about-the-author page, or a museum exhibition wall text — generous space, considered typography, an off-white paper feel. Light theme always.

**Anti-references** (the user explicitly excluded these — never reach for them):

- ❌ Generic stock photography — no smiling-people-in-suits, no hands-holding, no candles-and-leaves wellness imagery, no abstract "calm landscape" hero photos.
- ❌ Corporate-blue + gray-card + checkmark-list trust signals (the visual language every Brazilian clinic and law firm uses).
- ❌ Over-the-top decorative motion — no floating particles, no parallax-everywhere, no cinematic page-load intros.
- ❌ Sage-green-as-dominant-color (the saturated mental-health visual cliché). The client mentioned a possible teal-sage logo but is **not committed to honoring it**; treat any greens as a possible _accent_, not a foundation.

**Positive direction**:

- **Color**: Warm neutrals carry the page — bone, parchment, putty, soft clay. The 10% accent is a single deep, considered color (a deep oxblood, an aged ink, or a quiet sage-teal pulled from the logo if it survives review). Never a gradient on text. Never neon. Never the AI-cyan-on-dark palette.
- **Typography**: A distinctive serif anchors the brand voice — but **not** Fraunces, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson, Playfair, Cormorant, or DM Serif (these are the AI defaults). Look at PP Editorial New, ABC Diatype, Söhne, Reckless, Migra, GT Sectra, Tiempos, or a Velvetyne face. Pair with a quiet, slightly mechanical sans for body and UI. **Two families maximum.**
- **Layout**: Asymmetric, left-aligned, with a clear measured rhythm. Let one element (a name, a sentence, a photograph if there is one) own the page. Cap body line length at 65–75ch. Use whitespace as a material, not a leftover.
- **Detail**: A _single_ handmade gesture — a hand-drawn underline on Lucas's name, an off-grid pull-quote, a subtly imperfect divider — appearing once or twice. Resist the urge to repeat it. Restraint is the point.
- **Motion**: Small, slow, exponential easing. Reveal-on-scroll for one or two key elements, nothing more. Reduced-motion users get a fully static page with no information loss.

### Design Principles

1. **Treat the visitor as a thinking adult, not a patient.** No infantilizing imagery, no over-explaining, no condescending warmth. The tone is one practitioner speaking calmly to one prospective client.
2. **Warmth lives in the material, not in the rounded corners.** Paper-feel backgrounds, considered type, generous breathing room. Not a Big Blob Of Soft Color, not pillowy cards stacked on pastel fields.
3. **Type does the heavy lifting.** The site earns its silence through typographic care. If a section feels weak, the answer is almost always _better hierarchy_, not _more decoration_.
4. **Greens are a guest, not a host.** If we use a sage-teal accent at all, it appears sparingly — a link state, a punctuation mark, a single divider. Warm neutrals own the page.
5. **Reduce friction at the contact moment.** The visitor came to make first contact. Every layout decision should make that moment feel safer and lower-stakes — clear language, visible form, no flourish that could read as performative.

### Technical Constraints

- pt-BR is the only shipping locale. Hardcoded strings in pt-BR; new copy goes through `src/messages/pt.json` when it makes sense.
- Architecture layers (`core` / `lib` / `ui` / `app`) are CI-enforced — see project `CLAUDE.md`.
- WCAG AA contrast minimum on light theme; AAA preferred for body text given the audience.
- Reduced-motion support is mandatory, not optional, for this audience.
- Test rendering with `PAYLOAD_ENABLED=false` — pages must tolerate missing CMS data.
